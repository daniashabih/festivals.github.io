Katra Ahluwalia

In busy Katra Ahluwalia, thousands of Sikhs make pilgrimages to the domed Golden Temple, built on water. Vast park JallianwalaBagh commemorates the hundreds of Indians shot by British troops in the 1919 Amritsar massacre, and archives and oral histories in the Partition Museum depict India’s 1947 mass migration. Buzzing bazaars offer Punjabi crafts and fabrics, while simple eateries serve Amritsari fish tikka.

Harmandir sahib

The Golden temple is located in the holy city of the Sikhs, Amritsar. The Golden temple is famous for its full golden dome, it is one of the most sacred pilgrim spots for Sikhs. The Mandir is built on a 67-ft square of marble and is a two storied structure.

Guru Granth Sahib

The Guru Granth Sahib (Punjabi: ਗੁਰੂ ਗ੍ਰੰਥ ਸਾਹਿਬ, pronounced [ɡʊɾuː ɡɾəntʰᵊ saːhɪb]) is the central holy religious scripture of Sikhism, regarded by Sikhs as the final, sovereign and eternal Guru following the lineage of the ten human gurus of the religion.

Anand Karaj

Anand Karaj (Punjabi: ਅਨੰਦ ਕਾਰਜ ānada kāraja) is the Sikh marriage ceremony, meaning "Act towards happiness" or "Act towards happy life", that was introduced by Guru Amar Das. The four laavaan (hymns which take place during the ceremony) were composed by his successor, Guru Ram Das.
